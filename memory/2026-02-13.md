# 2026-02-13 - Friday Research Session

## 9pm Bangkok Research Session

### 2026 SaaS Metrics Benchmarks (Fresh Data)

**Growth Rates:**
- Median bootstrapped growth: 23% annually
- Median VC-backed growth: 25% annually  
- Top performers: 40-50% growth
- Historical 60%+ hypergrowth era is over

**Net Revenue Retention (NRR) - The New North Star:**
- Median NRR: 106%
- Best-in-class: 120-130%
- Enterprise with network effects: 130%+
- **Critical insight:** Existing customers now generate 40%+ of new ARR (50%+ for companies above $50M)
- Companies with NRR >100% grow 1.5-3x faster than peers

**Gross Revenue Retention (GRR):**
- Target: 85-95%
- B2B SaaS should be >90%
- Below 85% = serious product/customer fit issue
- Below 80% = expansion revenue is just masking a gunshot wound

**Churn:**
- Average B2B SaaS churn: 4.9% annually
- "Good" monthly churn: <1% (5% annual)
- Best-in-class: <3% annually
- Higher ARPU customers churn less

**CAC Payback Period:**
- Early-stage: 8-12 months
- Mid-stage ($25M-$50M): 15-18 months
- Larger ($50M-$100M): 20-24 months
- Excellent benchmark: 12-15 months
- ACV >$100K median payback: 24 months

**LTV:CAC Ratio:**
- Minimum viable: 3:1
- Healthy: 4:1 (new rule of thumb)
- Excellent: 5:1+
- Too high (7:1+): Under-investing in growth

### Key Strategic Insights

**1. Retention > Acquisition in 2026**
- CAC rose 14% while growth slowed in 2025
- Market got smarter - customers harder to acquire
- But churn is improving while sales slow
- Winners = those who keep customers

**2. Vertical SaaS Gaining Traction**
- "Unlike horizontal SaaS, niche SaaS targets specific industries with less competition and higher customer loyalty"
- Construction, healthcare documentation, property management cited as hot verticals
- Premium pricing + customer stickiness

**3. Micro-SaaS Sweet Spot**
- 1-3 people teams
- Focus on "one killer workflow" not all-in-one
- Target: $1K-$20K MRR
- "Enough to quit your day job without conquering NASDAQ"

**4. AI Integration Non-Optional**
- 75% of SaaS companies will implement AI-driven automation by 2026
- 1 in 6 enterprise deals now involve AI capabilities
- AI must lower costs OR add value (not excessive)
