## 2026 Bootstrapping Framework (TAV Tech Synthesis)

### Why Bootstrap in 2026?
- Bootstrapped SaaS has **higher 5-year survival rate** than VC-funded
- Subscription fatigue is real → users cancel anything not delivering immediate value
- Revenue as lifeblood forces better decisions

### The 10-Step Playbook

**1. Solve a Small, Agonizing Problem**
- Question to answer: "Who will pay for this within 30 days?"
- Micro-SaaS > broad platforms
- Start with a **feature**, not a platform

**2. Validate with Revenue, Not Feedback**
- 5 paying customers > 100 interested signups
- Pre-access sales, paid pilots, paid beta plans
- Bare bones landing page with **prices shown**

**3. MVP = Minimal, Not Half-Finished**
- One core problem solved very well
- Opinionated, not flexible
- Feature restraint = discipline
- "Many bootstrapped SaaS fail from over-building"

**4. Price Early, Price Honestly**
- Low prices = signals instability in 2026
- 2-3 tiers max, clear usage metrics
- Price on outcomes: time saved, errors reduced, revenue unlocked
- Don't delay price increases (early customers expect it)

**5. Revenue Before Team**
- Hire late, slowly, intentionally
- Founders should handle: sales, support, product
- Closeness to users = superpower
- Hire only for: direct revenue, founder bottleneck removal, churn reduction

**6. Compounding Growth Channels**
- NO paid ads until messaging/conversion proven
- 2026 compounding channels:
  - High-intent SEO content
  - Community around role/problem
  - Partnerships with complementary tools
  - Founder-led outbound
- **Channel depth > channel spread**

**7. Retention = Growth Strategy**
- CAC is high in 2026 → retention matters more
- Onboarding, documentation, proactive support
- Open communication builds loyalty

**8. AI: Strategic, Not Excessive**
- AI on backburner: speed + accuracy, not complexity
- Smart uses: automation, data analysis, support reduction
- AI must lower costs OR add value (customers detect BS)

**9. Cash Flow Visibility**
- Monitor MRR religiously
- Track churn obsessively
- Conservative cost projection
- Profitability ≠ dirty word (creates flexibility)

**10. Raise Only When Proven**
- Bootstrap ≠ never raise
- Raise only when: model proven, unit economics healthy
- Leverage = voluntary funding on your terms
