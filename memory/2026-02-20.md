**Source:** Business of Apps / Designli survey of founders, product leaders, operators

| Founder Type | Focus Areas |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Technical** | Architecture impact, compute costs, data accessibility |
| **Non-technical** | User value creation, differentiation, reliability concerns |

**Where AI Actually Helps Most (ranked by founder priority):**

1. **Internal automation** â€” billing, reconciliation, QA, admin tasks

3. **Smarter onboarding** â€” adaptive guidance, context-aware recommendations

**Top Concerns About AI Adoption:**

- Clear user demand / repeated requests
- Ties to measurable outcomes (time saved, costs reduced)
- Clean, reliable data + clear integration path
- Supports existing workflow vs. introducing new one

**Validation Methods (ranked):**
1. Internal dogfooding (catch issues early)
2. Limited betas (observe real user behavior)
3. Public iteration (shorten feedback cycles)

**Critical Insight:** AI is rarely cited as primary differentiator. Teams focus on:

### The Four Filters Every SaaS Idea Must Pass (2026)

**Source:** OyeLabs analysis

Before building, validate against:

| Filter | Question | Pass Criteria |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| **Pain Frequency** | How often does problem occur? | Weekly or daily (annual = bad) |
| **Buyer Clarity** | Who exactly is the buyer? | Specific role, not "businesses" |
| **Automation Potential** | Can AI/automation reduce effort? | Yes, meaningfully |
| **Willingness to Pay** | Will they pay monthly? | Recurring value exists |

> Ideas failing even ONE filter often become stalled MVPs.

**2026 Reality:** Buyers shop for *relief*, not tools. They want:
- Removed manual work
- Reduced decision fatigue
- Improved speed, accuracy, visibility

### Framework: Launch-First Thinking

**From OyeLabs:**

> "In 2026, launching a SaaS isn't about having a big idea. It's about having the *right* idea, building fast, and shipping before the market moves on."

**The smart path:**

3. Scale (based on evidence)
